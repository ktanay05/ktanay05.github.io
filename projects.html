Projects

During my undergrad years, I was deeply involved in aeromodelling and aerial robotics competitions—basically, if it flew, I wanted to build it. That hands-on excitement hasn't quite left me; these days, I still tinker and fly for fun, though my focus has gradually shifted toward more research-driven, academic projects.

What has stayed constant is my fascination with building systems that matter—projects that push boundaries and have real-world impact. Along this journey, I've had the privilege of working alongside brilliant peers and mentors who have shaped not just my work, but the way I think about problems.

Below is a look at some of the projects I have contributed to, with the most recent one being at the top.

FPGA based GNSS receiver development ▼

In this R&D project, we aimed to develop a highly configurable custom GNSS receiver for precision guidance of high spin projectiles. The rotating antenna and the high spin velocity of the projectile make it difficult to obtain a satellite lock. Hardware requirements are also sophisticated due to the high g-force acting on the projectile. Hence satellite-based navigation is a challenging task. Custom algorithms have been employed to optimize the FPGA core utilization, increasing the overall performance.

Autonomous Tiltrotor UAV ▼

The project aimed to develop a versatile autonomous tiltrotor UAV with numerous applications. I took this project as a part of the student project team AeroMIT while pursuing my undergraduate degree. My work was primarily focused on flight controls, avionics and manufacturing of the UAV to ensure a stable flight. A simple PID control algorithm was used for the attitude tracking loop. Because of the unconventional configuration, ensuring flight stability was a very challenging task.
