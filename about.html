About Me

Aviation and aerospace have fascinated me since my school days. While my initial aspirations leaned towards the corporate aviation industry, my undergraduate years profoundly reshaped my perspective leaning me towards research and development. My exploration of various domains—from aerodynamics to avionics—eventually led me to aerospace Guidance, Navigation, and Control (GNC) and robotics, where I found my true passion. My tenure at IIT Kanpur solidified this commitment, setting me on a path to push the boundaries of aerospace autonomy.

Currently, I am a Ph.D. student in Aerospace Engineering at Texas A&M University, where I am a member of the Intelligent Systems Research (ISR) Lab, working under the guidance of Prof. Raktim Bhattacharya. With a background in Electronics and Communication Engineering from Manipal Institute of Technology, I bring a unique perspective to aerospace systems, particularly in designing and analyzing complex avionics, communication protocols, and control architectures essential for autonomous aerospace applications. My research focuses on modular robotics, optimization, robust control, and fault-tolerant systems—key areas that drive innovation in aerospace autonomy.

Throughout my journey, I have been fortunate to gain hands-on experience at esteemed institutions, including IIT Bombay, IIT Kanpur, and the National Technical Research Organization (NTRO). My work has spanned diverse projects, from satellite-based navigation and UAV swarm development to robust control of aerial vehicles. As a researcher at Texas A&M, I continue to bridge the gap between theoretical advancements and practical implementation, striving to develop efficient and intelligent aerospace systems. Additionally, as a teaching assistant, I take great pride in mentoring and guiding students in aerospace engineering.

Beyond academics, I believe in maintaining a well-rounded life. I enjoy playing the ukulele, badminton, and cooking—creative outlets that complement my technical pursuits. I have also actively contributed to community service, leading workshops, organizing student events, and engaging in environmental initiatives.

My journey so far has been shaped by curiosity, mentorship, and an unwavering passion for aerospace. This is just the beginning of an exciting path, and I hope there is enough time for everything!
